# 09 — Go-To-Market and Creator Marketplace

## Launch posture

Efi-G should launch as a private, invite-led avatar studio with a visible creator waitlist. The marketplace should feel inevitable, but not fully open on day one.

## Market narrative

AI media is moving from prompts to identity rights. The winning platform will not be the one that generates the most images; it will be the one users and creators trust with permissioned digital selves.

## Initial positioning

**Create your avatar. Keep your control. License only what you choose.**

## Launch audience

### Phase 1 — controlled beta

- AI-curious adults.
- Digital creators.
- Independent performers and fan-community builders.
- Artists experimenting with self-image and fantasy characters.
- Early adopters comfortable with private AI tools.

### Phase 2 — creator supply

- Mid-tier creators with loyal audiences.
- Cosplay, fashion, glamour, fitness, fantasy, and roleplay creators.
- Digital artists with character IP.
- Streamers and community hosts.

### Phase 3 — marketplace demand

- Fans who want controlled access to creator-approved avatars.
- Users who want avatar packs, rooms, and custom scenarios.
- Partners with accessory and environment packs.

## Creator acquisition strategy

### Subsidized seed creators

Offer selected creators subsidized avatar setup in exchange for:

- launch participation;
- case study rights;
- limited promotional exclusivity;
- feedback sessions;
- early revenue-share testing.

### Creator pitch

Efi-G gives creators a governed way to monetize AI avatar access while preserving boundaries, pricing, and revocation rights.

### Creator onboarding funnel

1. Application.
2. Identity and rights review.
3. Content/category boundary selection.
4. Reference capture guide.
5. Avatar training or setup.
6. Quality review.
7. Storefront draft.
8. Launch campaign.
9. Analytics review.

## Marketplace mechanics

### Listing types

- Creator avatar access.
- Character packs.
- Prompt packs.
- Environment packs.
- Accessory packs.
- Private room templates.
- Limited campaign drops.

### License forms

- Self-only.
- Fan/customer pack.
- Collaboration license.
- Creator-reviewed license.
- Limited-time campaign.
- Premium custom request.

## Growth loops

### User loop

Private generation creates outputs. Outputs create desire for better avatars, more credits, and creator packs.

### Creator loop

Creator storefront creates fan demand. Fan demand funds better creator onboarding. Better creators improve marketplace reputation.

### Asset loop

Popular avatars create demand for environments and accessories. Asset creators join to monetize add-ons. Add-ons increase generation spend.

## Promotion packages

- Featured creator launch.
- Category homepage placement.
- Drop campaign.
- Affiliate boost.
- Partner bundle.
- Creator collab spotlight.

## Metrics

- Creator application volume.
- Approved creator rate.
- Creator setup cost.
- Creator first-sale rate.
- Average revenue per creator.
- Customer repeat purchase rate.
- Gross marketplace value.
- Platform take rate.
- Promotion package conversion.
- Refund and dispute rate.

## Launch campaigns

### Campaign 1 — The Private Avatar Studio

Focus on the user: create expressive images with your own controlled avatar.

### Campaign 2 — Creator Boundary Economy

Focus on creators: monetize avatar access without giving up control.

### Campaign 3 — Rooms Preview

Show the future: governed spaces where avatars, characters, and assets combine into interactive experiences.

## Strategic warning

Do not chase viral growth before compliance, rights enforcement, and payment stability are proven. This category will punish sloppy governance.
