# 02 — Business Model

## Strategic model

Efi-G begins as a private avatar studio, then expands into a rights-governed creator marketplace. Revenue comes from generation credits, avatar hosting, creator licensing, marketplace discovery, premium privacy controls, and promotion packages.

## Customer segments

### 1. Personal users

Users who want low-cost private generation involving their own avatar or fictional characters.

Needs: simple onboarding, low-cost credits, strong privacy controls, fast generation, prompt presets, and a private vault.

### 2. Creators

Creators who want to monetize controlled avatar access without losing ownership or control.

Needs: verified profiles, avatar training support, license scopes, pricing tiers, usage analytics, payout reporting, revocation controls, and storefront tooling.

### 3. Customers and fans

Customers who want access to creator-approved avatar experiences.

Needs: clear limits, affordable packs, trusted outputs, private purchase flow, and predictable boundaries.

### 4. Asset partners

Accessory, environment, character, and IP owners who want licensed digital objects or worlds available in the marketplace.

Needs: rights controls, revenue share, brand safety, usage analytics, and asset packaging.

## Revenue lines

| Revenue line | Description | Launch priority |
|---|---|---:|
| Generation credits | Pay-as-you-go image generation | 1 |
| Subscription tiers | Monthly credit bundles and privacy features | 1 |
| Creator avatar hosting | Paid creator model storage, tuning, and storefront | 2 |
| Marketplace take rate | Platform fee on licensed avatar usage | 2 |
| Promotion packages | Paid visibility, featured drops, and launch campaigns | 3 |
| Asset packs | Environments, props, styles, and room templates | 3 |
| IP licensing | Licensed character libraries and custom brand spaces | 4 |
| Video credits | Higher-cost generation once reliability improves | 4 |
| Interactive rooms | Paid room access and scenario orchestration | 4 |

## Recommended pricing draft

### User tier

- Free: small monthly trial credits, preview mode, private-only outputs.
- Plus: monthly credits, higher resolution, personal avatar storage.
- Pro: larger credit bundle, faster queue, private vault controls.

### Creator tier

- Creator Starter: profile, storefront waitlist, basic analytics.
- Creator Pro: avatar hosting, custom license scopes, paid packs.
- Creator Partner: subsidized onboarding for high-demand creators in exchange for launch exclusivity or platform promotional rights.

### Marketplace take rate

- Standard creator sales: 20% to 30% platform fee.
- Partner creators: negotiated lower fee, usually 10% to 20%.
- Paid promotion: flat fee or auction-based visibility.

## Unit economics to track

- Cost per generation by model profile.
- Average revenue per generation.
- Failed generation rate.
- Refund rate.
- Chargeback rate.
- Review cost per 1,000 jobs.
- Creator onboarding cost.
- Creator gross merchandise value.
- Marketplace take rate.
- Storage cost per avatar.
- GPU utilization and queue latency.

## Launch economics principle

Never subsidize heavy generation without understanding GPU cost per completed output. Use limited free credits, strict rate limits, queue priority, and model tiers so experimentation cannot create an uncontrolled infrastructure bill.

## Payment strategy

The platform should plan for specialized payment infrastructure from day one. Payment research must include processor category rules, chargeback mitigation, payout compliance, rolling reserves, and account stability.

## Defensible moat

The moat is not only model quality. It is the combination of rights-governed generation, creator-owned avatar licensing, consent and revocation ledger, privacy-first architecture, curated marketplace supply, policy-aware model routing, payment competence, and eventual interactive room dynamics.
